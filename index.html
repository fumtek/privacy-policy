Impri Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-13

Who we are

Impri (“the App”) is a photo stylization application for iOS. This Privacy Policy explains how the App handles information when you use it.

Summary

  • Photo editing and gallery storage happen on your device.
  • The App does not sell your personal data.
  • The App does not use third-party advertising or tracking SDKs for cross-app tracking.
  • Optional In-App Purchase (Impri Plus) is processed by Apple.

Photos and media

When you grant Photo Library permission, the App may:

  • Read photos you choose to edit
  • Save edited images back to your Photo Library (if you allow)
  • Keep gallery masters and thumbnails in the App’s storage (including an App Group container shared with the Impri share extension)

Images you import from other apps via Share / Action Extension are processed to open an editing session. Some metadata may be lost when another app re-encodes the image before sharing.

Gallery data

The App stores original/edited image files and related metadata (for example, work records and variant information) locally on your device using Apple frameworks such as SwiftData and the file system. Deleting the App removes this on-device data, subject to iOS backup behavior.

Purchases

If you buy Impri Plus, purchase and restore are handled by Apple through StoreKit. We do not receive your full payment card details. Apple’s terms and privacy policy also apply to purchases.

Language preference

If you choose an in-app language, that preference may be stored in App Group UserDefaults so the main app and extension stay consistent.

Analytics and tracking

The App does not use App Tracking Transparency for cross-app tracking, and does not include third-party ad networks in the current build described by this policy.

Children

The App is not directed at children under 13. Do not use the App if you are under the minimum age required in your region for App Store use.

Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy. The “Last updated” date will change when we do. Continued use after an update means you accept the revised policy, where permitted by law.
